# Question Patterns
Version 1.0

This document defines reusable question patterns for original Year 4 reading assessments.

All questions must be original and must not copy copyrighted NFER wording.

---

## 1. Domain Coverage

Each test should roughly include:

- 3 Retrieval questions
- 2 Vocabulary questions
- 4 Inference questions
- 1 Structure question
- 2 Author Intent questions

---

## 2. Retrieval Patterns

### R1. Find the stated fact
Example stem:
- Where did Sam find the key?
- What tool did the scientists use?
- When did the storm begin?

Recommended format:
- MCQ or short answer

Skill tag:
- scanning for facts

---

### R2. Identify which statement is true
Example stem:
- Which statement is true?
- Which of these does the text tell us?

Recommended format:
- MCQ

Skill tag:
- identifying stated information

---

### R3. Locate evidence
Example stem:
- Which sentence shows that Mina was nervous?
- Find one word that tells you the cave was dark.

Recommended format:
- evidence selection or short answer

Skill tag:
- locating evidence

---

## 3. Vocabulary Patterns

### V1. Meaning in context
Example stem:
- What does the word "hesitated" mean in this sentence?
- Which word is closest in meaning to "glimmered"?

Recommended format:
- MCQ

Skill tag:
- meaning in context

---

### V2. Shade of meaning
Example stem:
- Why did the writer use the word "stared" instead of "looked"?
- What does "crept" suggest about how he moved?

Recommended format:
- short answer

Skill tag:
- shades of meaning

---

### V3. Pronoun reference
Example stem:
- What does the word "it" refer to?
- Who does "they" refer to in paragraph 3?

Recommended format:
- short answer or MCQ

Skill tag:
- pronoun reference

---

## 4. Inference Patterns

### I1. Character feeling
Example stem:
- How was Ava feeling at this point?
- What does this tell you about Ben’s feelings?

Recommended format:
- short answer

Model answer guidance:
- feeling + evidence

Skill tag:
- character feelings

---

### I2. Motive
Example stem:
- Why did the character hide the letter?
- Why did the captain refuse to go closer?

Recommended format:
- short answer

Skill tag:
- motives

---

### I3. Cause and effect
Example stem:
- Why did the path become harder to follow?
- What caused the animals to leave the area?

Recommended format:
- short answer

Skill tag:
- cause and effect

---

### I4. Multi-cue inference
Example stem:
- How can you tell that the place felt dangerous?
Use two details from the text.

Recommended format:
- 2-mark short answer

Skill tag:
- implied meaning

---

## 7. Marking Rules

### 1 mark questions
Award 1 mark for:
- correct answer
- or acceptable equivalent wording

### 2 mark questions
Award 2 marks for:
- correct idea
- supported by evidence or explanation

Award 1 mark for:
- plausible idea without evidence
- or partial explanation

### 3 mark questions
Award 3 marks for:
- clear explanation
- relevant evidence
- direct link to the question focus

---

## 8. Model Answer Format

Each open question should include:

- Gold answer
- Silver answer
- Common wrong answer

Example:

Question:
Why did Mia hesitate before entering the cave?

Gold:
Mia hesitated because she felt nervous about what might be inside and was not sure if the cave was safe.

Silver:
She was nervous.

Common wrong answer:
She wanted to go home.

Why wrong:
This is not supported by the text.

---

## 9. Quality Rules

Do:
- keep wording clear
- test one main skill at a time
- use British English
- vary stems across the question bank
- make distractors plausible in MCQs

Do not:
- write trick questions
- include two correct MCQ answers
- rely on background knowledge outside the passage
- make every open response require a quote

---

## 10. Difficulty Guide

### Difficulty 1
- direct retrieval
- simple vocabulary
- one-sentence answers

### Difficulty 2
- retrieval across nearby sentences
- straightforward inference
- familiar vocabulary

### Difficulty 3
- implied feelings and motives
- paragraph main idea
- effect of simple word choices

### Difficulty 4
- multi-part inference
- tone and author intent
- comparison across parts of the text

### Difficulty 5
- subtle viewpoint
- complex authorial choices
- abstract interpretation

Year 4 should mainly use:
- difficulty 2
- difficulty 3
- some difficulty 4
