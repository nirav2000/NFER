# Content Guidelines
Version 1.0

These guidelines ensure high-quality reading passages and questions.

---

# Passage Writing

Passages must be:

- clear and engaging
- suitable for Year 4 readers
- written in British English
- 300–450 words

Avoid:

- complex adult vocabulary
- overly simple sentences
- repetitive phrasing

---

# Fiction Passages

Should include:

- character
- setting
- small narrative event
- emotional cues

Example themes:

- exploration
- mystery
- animals
- school adventures
- nature

---

# Nonfiction Passages

Should include:

- factual information
- structured paragraphs
- clear explanations

Example topics:

- weather
- space
- wildlife
- inventions
- geography

---

# Question Quality Rules

Each question must:

- refer clearly to the text
- avoid ambiguity
- test a specific reading skill

Avoid:

- trick questions
- vague wording
- multiple correct answers

---

# Model Answers

Provide:

Gold answer  
Silver answer  
Common incorrect answer

Example:

Gold:
The character hesitated because she felt nervous about entering the cave.

Silver:
She was scared.

Incorrect:
She was tired.

---

# Difficulty Guidelines

Level 1:
simple retrieval

Level 2:
basic vocabulary

Level 3:
simple inference

Level 4:
multi-sentence reasoning

Level 5:
author intent and tone

Year 4 should use levels **2–4**.

---

# Passage Variety

Avoid repeating themes.

Rotate topics such as:

- oceans
- deserts
- forests
- animals
- inventions
- explorers
